EV Car

An electric vehicle(EV) is a type of car that runs on electricity instead of traditional fuels like petrol or diesel. It uses a battery and an electric motor to operate, making it more environmentally friendly as it produces little to no pollution or greenhouse gas emissions. EVs also have lower maintenance costs since they don’t require engine oil or complex mechanical parts found in conventional vehicles. Governments around the world are promoting EVs by offering subsidies, tax benefits, and improving charging infrastructure. In the future, electric cars are expected to become a cleaner and more sustainable alternative to traditional vehicles.

    EV car parts Function

    Name Function
    Electric Motor Converts electrical energy from the battery into mechanical energy to move the car’s wheels. It replaces the engine found in petrol or diesel cars.
    Onboard Charger Converts AC power from a charging station into DC power to charge the battery. It also manages the battery charging rate safely.
    Inverter Converts DC (Direct Current) from the battery into AC (Alternating Current) for the motor. It ensures the motor runs efficiently.
    Controller Controls the flow of power from the battery to the motor. It adjusts speed, torque, and acceleration depending on how the driver presses the accelerator.
